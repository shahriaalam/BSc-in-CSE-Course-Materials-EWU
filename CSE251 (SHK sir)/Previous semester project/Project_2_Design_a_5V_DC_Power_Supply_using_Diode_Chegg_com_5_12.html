Design

Bridge rectifier selected as rectifier, 1N4007 common rectifier diodes are choosen for the same, the capacitor filter choosen 1000uF (1mF) high value capacitor for smooth filtering,and the capacitor filter charges to a voltage=12V-1.4=10V approximately, where 1.4V is the drop across two diodes while conducting in the bridge rectifier , and the voltage across the capacitor filter is fed to Zener diode through series resistance, here the resistance is choosen such that,

Rs=10V-5.1V(Vz)/75mA=65

here 75mA is taken just for an example such that load current be 50mA , and to maintain a minimum zener current,.for higher load currents the design can be altered, here the zener used is 5.1V zener 1N4733A having breakdown voltage 5.1V as we know the voltage applied to the zener diode here is greater than its breakdown voltage it keeps the zener diode in breakdown region as such it regulates the unregulated input DC voltage and produces a regulated DC of 5V across load.

The load resistor value choosen such a way that load current IL=50mA

RL=Vz/IL=5.1V/50mA=100

Simulation and implementation of the design in Multisim

we can observe from the above 5V regulated output obtained across load .

please note that in mulisim input given is in peak tnat is why 12V , So that peak to peak of 24V and as the frequency not specified given 60Hz.
